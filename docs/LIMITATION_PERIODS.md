# DeadlineWA — Limitation Periods Reference Data

All periods below are from the relevant Limitation Acts of each jurisdiction.
Periods are in YEARS unless otherwise noted. "Accrual" means the date the
cause of action arose (not the date of the event, unless they're the same).

## Important Notes

1. **Discoverability**: in WA, NSW, Vic, SA, Tas the limitation period for personal injury runs from when the plaintiff knew (or ought to have known) of the injury, cause, and defendant — NOT from the date of the event.

2. **Disabilities**: limitation periods may be extended if the plaintiff was under a disability (minor, person of unsound mind) at the time of accrual. DeadlineWA notes this but does NOT calculate extensions — flag for manual review.

3. **Acknowledgment / Part Payment**: in debt claims, an acknowledgment or part payment restarts the limitation period. DeadlineWA notes this but does NOT track it.

4. **Fraud / Concealment**: limitation periods may be extended where the defendant concealed the cause of action. DeadlineWA notes this.

5. **NT is shorter**: the Northern Territory has 3-year limitation periods where most states have 6 years. This catches people out.

6. **Federal matters**: there is no single Commonwealth limitations act. Federal limitation periods are scattered across specific statutes (*Competition and Consumer Act*, *Corporations Act*, etc.).

## WA Public Holidays (for working day calculations)

### Fixed dates (every year)
- New Year's Day: 1 January
- Australia Day: 26 January
- Anzac Day: 25 April
- Christmas Day: 25 December
- Boxing Day: 26 December

### Variable dates (computed)
- Good Friday: Friday before Easter Sunday (Computus algorithm)
- Saturday before Easter Sunday
- Easter Monday: Monday after Easter Sunday
- WA Day: 1st Monday in June
- King's Birthday (WA): 4th Monday in September (NOTE: WA is DIFFERENT — most states use 2nd Monday in June)

### Substitute day rules (WA)
- If a fixed holiday falls on Saturday → Monday is the public holiday
- If a fixed holiday falls on Sunday → Monday is the public holiday
- If both Christmas and Boxing Day fall on Sat/Sun → Mon and Tue are public holidays
